# Celestial Crown Design Philosophy

## Core Identity

Celestial Crown is a squad-based, real-time strategic RPG focused on command decisions rather than per-attack micromanagement. The player wins by planning, composition, deployment timing, route control, and pressure management across multiple fronts.

The game should feel like commanding a living field army where outcomes are earned before the clash begins.

## Player Role and Decision Focus

The player is a field commander and strategist.

Primary decisions:
- Build and restructure squads for purpose.
- Assign movement and objectives on a live map.
- Control map tempo and reinforcement lanes.
- Decide when to press, hold, rotate, and retreat.
- Convert territorial control into campaign momentum.

The player is not expected to manually issue each strike once squads engage.

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Build and modify squads.
2. Deploy squads onto a real-time strategic map.
3. Move, intercept, and react to enemy pressure.
4. Trigger auto-resolved squad engagements.
5. Capture and defend strategic sites.
6. Gain income, experience, and campaign position.
7. Upgrade units and reform squad roles.
8. Enter the next mission with stronger but constrained options.

## Strategic Pillars

### 1) Map Control Is Power
Strategic sites are not cosmetic. Controlled sites materially affect:
- Income and economy.
- Recovery and reinforcement opportunities.
- Travel safety and lane pressure.
- Access to deployment and staging space.

Losing ground should create cascading risk. Holding ground should create strategic leverage.

### 2) Squad Identity Matters
A squad is the primary combat actor on the strategic map. Each squad should have a clear purpose:
- Assault breakthrough.
- Defensive anchor.
- Hunter/interceptor.
- Support and sustain.
- Mobility and flank pressure.

Depth comes from meaningful role specialization and matchup clarity.

### 3) Preparation Drives Combat Outcomes
Engagements are auto-resolved and should reflect prior decisions:
- Composition and role fit.
- Front/back row and leader strength.
- Stat spread, class synergies, and growth.
- Tactics settings and target priorities.

The player should understand why a squad won or lost and connect that result back to map and roster decisions.

### 4) Persistent Army Development
Campaign progression should reward long-term thinking:
- Units level, specialize, and evolve roles.
- Squad effectiveness grows through synergy, not level alone.
- Resource constraints force tradeoffs between breadth, quality, and tempo.

Progression is strategic expression, not only numeric inflation.

### 5) Multi-Front Pressure and Tempo
The game should create sustained command pressure:
- Enemy squads move in real time and threaten weak lanes.
- Safe routes can collapse if ignored.
- The player is regularly balancing offense and stabilization.

Good play is about prioritization and timing under incomplete comfort.

## Campaign and World Layer Principles

- Missions are connected through persistent army state.
- Town/base management should feed strategic choices, not distract from them.
- Story and morality systems should influence recruitment, class access, and outcomes where possible.
- The army should feel ideological as well as tactical over time.

## UI Principles

### Strategic Map Readability
The player must quickly parse:
- Site ownership and front lines.
- Ally and enemy squad movement.
- Objective pressure and intercept risks.
- Which lanes are stable or collapsing.

### Squad Readability
The player must quickly inspect:
- Role and intended purpose.
- Unit composition and leader status.
- Health state and readiness.
- Why matchup outcomes are favorable or risky.

### Combat Readability
Auto-resolve presentation should show:
- Who is acting and in what order.
- Momentum swings and role interactions.
- Causal links from composition/tactic decisions to battle outcomes.

## Emotional Rhythm Targets

The intended rhythm alternates between:
- Strategic tension on the map (pressure, split attention, timing decisions).
- Management clarity between battles (rebuilding, planning, optimization).
- Outcome payoff in engagements (seeing decisions validated or punished).

Attachment should form around both individual units and whole squad identities.

## Scope Guardrails

To preserve identity, avoid drifting into:
- Full manual tactics-grid micromanagement of every strike.
- Pure RTS click-spam without RPG persistence and squad identity.
- Pure menu RPG progression detached from map pressure.

Celestial Crown should stay in the hybrid middle: strategic command, squad doctrine, and long-arc RPG growth.
